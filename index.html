▸ 00 / Operating Principles

Six rules every flow obeys.

The strategic frame. Before timing, before channels, before copy — these are the rails. No flow ships without them.

01

Indoctrinate before pitch

The first three emails of any acquisition flow build identity, story, and worldview before asking for anything. Skip this and you train the list to delete you.

▸ Brunson Soap Opera
02

Soap opera before sale

Each touch builds on the previous. Open loops, callbacks, story arcs. Treat the sequence like an episode, not seven isolated emails.

▸ Brunson · DotCom Secrets
03

Reward before ask

Hormozi "give til they ask" — every email rewards the open with value (insight, story, social proof). The ask is earned by the time it lands.

▸ Hormozi · $100M Offers
04

The grand slam is the close

When you make the ask, make it so valuable they'd feel stupid saying no. Stack value, reduce friction, reverse risk. Average offers get average results.

▸ Hormozi · Grand Slam Offer
05

SMS is a privilege

Used sparingly, SMS feels like a personal text. Used like email, it gets you blocked. 1–2 SMS per flow. Time-sensitive only. Quiet hours respected.

▸ Channel Discipline
06

Decision-force the final touch

The last email in any sequence should make it easier to say no than to ignore. Counter-intuitively, this drives the most conversions and cleans the list.

▸ Brunson + Hormozi
